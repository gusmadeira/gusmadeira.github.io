About Me

gtvmadeira@gmail.com

madeira@ipgp.fr

I hold a PhD in Physics from São Paulo State University (Brazil) and was a postdoctoral researcher at the Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris (France) from 2023 to 2025. My research focuses on contributing to the understanding of the formation, evolution, and composition of Solar System objects, as well as those in exoplanetary systems. I have a solid background in studying the origin and dynamics of satellites, rings, and minor bodies. My research primarily involves numerical simulations. I have experience with various computational tools, including N-body codes, hydrodynamics codes, and SPH codes. Furthermore, I am deeply interested in orbital dynamics theory and strive to compare my numerical results with theoretical predictions and observational data. I have been part of NASA's DART mission, and I am currently a member of ESA's Hera mission. My contributions to the field have been recognized with the Wagner Sessin Award in Dynamical and Planetary Astronomy and the Best Thesis Award from São Paulo State University.

Research Topics

  • Composition: composition of the Moon and exoplanet atmospheres.
  • Giant impacts: formation of the Moon, formation of Phobos and Deimos, and exoplanet formation.
  • Perturbations on small bodies: solar radiation, plasma drag, Lorentz forces, tides, and Yarkovsky effect.
  • Ring dynamics: stability and formation of planetary rings and arcs.
  • Satellite formation: accretion in circumplanetary disks and rings, pebble accretion, gas-driven migration, tides, and ring torques.
  • Small bodies: formation of binary systems, rings, and dynamics around small bodies.
  • Small impacts: dust production due to meteoroid impacts.
