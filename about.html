I hold a PhD in Physics from São Paulo State University (Brazil) and am currently completing a two-year postdoctoral fellowship at the Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris (France). My research focuses on contributing to the understanding of the formation, evolution, and composition of Solar System objects, as well as those in exo-solar systems. I have a solid background in studying the formation and dynamics of satellites and Solar System minor bodies. Additionally, my current work at a cosmochemistry lab has strengthened my connection to the field of cosmochemistry. My research primarily involves numerical simulations. I have extensive experience with various computational tools, including N-body codes, hydrodynamics codes, and SPH codes. Furthermore, I am deeply interested in orbital dynamics theory and strive to compare my numerical results with theoretical predictions and observational data. I have been part of NASA's DART mission and currently I am a member of the ESA's Hera mission. My contributions to the field have been recognized with the Wagner Sessin Awards in Dynamical and Planetary Astronomy and the Best Thesis Award from São Paulo State University.

Research Topics

  • Satellite formation: accretion in circumplanetary disks and rings, pebble accretion, gas-driven migration, tides, and ring torques.
  • Giant impacts: formation of the Moon, formation of Phobos and Deimos.
  • Composition: composition of the Moon, exoplanets' atmosphere.
  • Small bodies: formation of binary systems, rings, and dynamics around small bodies.
  • Perturbations on small bodies: solar radiation, plasma drag, Lorentz forces, tides, and Yarkovsky effect.
  • Ring dynamics: stability and formation of planetary rings and arcs.
  • Small impacts: dust production due to meteoroid impacts.
